Cannon 130-137 are formed on 8 granite boulders set in a roughly straight line extending 35 metres on western flank of the defence.  See Vol.II Central Survey & Inventory.RCAHMW&M (1170)p.175 
Cannon 138 - 143 have been formed as seperate Cannon sites adjacent to each other on granite outcrop. 
Cannon 144 is located on W.of roadside between Llanberis and Nant Peris 
Cannon 169 - 172 are formed on large heavily weathered shaly slate slabs, the holes of varying depth and diameter. Much evidence of delamination on slab surfaces during firing of cannon, and subsequent deepening of some holes. Cannon No.4 has the initials ‘A H’  carved on its surface. 
Post War Cannon did not require holes as did the traditional cannon, these cannon were formed along redundant slate tips,and consisted of scores of half sticks of dynamite fitted with plain detonators and short lengths of safety fuses, the free ends of which were laid in the channel of flat bottomed tramway rails laid on their sides,a fuse train of black powder then laid along the channel to connect all the safety fuses. This type of cannon did not produce the spectacular visual effects of the traditional cannon. 
Cannon 173 formed on a large boulder, has been moved from its original location to its present site during field clearing. 
Cannon 175 initially had 31 holes, but 4 were lost by fragmentation of the rock surface subsequent to its first firing. 
Cannon No.180 is located one Gallery directly above Cannon No.181. 
Cannon 185 is heavily inscribed with C P for Capel Peniel. 
Cannon 198 clearly predates the building of the main exit incline in 1850, the cannon having been partly destroyed by forming the access cutting to the incline head. 
Cannon 204 made and fired to celebrate the 60th birthday of Dr Michael J.T.Lewis on the 30th August, 1998. 
Cannon 214 drilled in naturally pitted limestone ridge overlooking bay. 
Cannon 218 consists of 4 holes and 3 partly drilled holes on top of a boulder above the farm yard. Cannon 225 At Llanbedr Village, at least 25 holes with grooves on several outcrops on top of a crag behind the mill, with probably more holes under the turf. 
Cannon 19 Was fired on midday, January 1st,2000 to celebrate the arrival of the third Millennium. NGR of Cannon 227 is an approximation. 
A site exists a few metres to the N/W of Cannon 227 that contains 12 holes which vary in depths up to 24". These are believed to have been used for firing 
rockets. 
Cannon 228 consists of 32 holes on two levels, of 20 and 12 holes, a short groove connects 3 holes. 
Cannon 230 a natural groove connects the holes. The NGR of Cannon 230 and 231 are approximations.
